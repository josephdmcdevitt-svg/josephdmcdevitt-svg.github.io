AI-Powered Private Equity Deal Sourcing Engine

An automated system that scrapes 8 business-for-sale marketplaces daily, runs a dual scoring algorithm (Operator vs. PE buyer), enriches with Census Bureau and BLS labor data, and surfaces 5,000+ ranked deals in a live dashboard.

Problem

PE deal sourcing is manual and slow. Analysts waste hours scanning fragmented platforms, copying data into spreadsheets, and applying inconsistent criteria. Good deals get buried before they're even reviewed — and there's no way to objectively compare an owner-operator opportunity against a PE platform target.

Solution

A zero-touch daily pipeline that scrapes listings from 8 broker networks, deduplicates across sources, runs two independent 0–100 scoring frameworks (solo buyer vs. PE sponsor), enriches each deal with public market data from the Census Bureau and Bureau of Labor Statistics, and delivers a ranked pipeline with full scoring breakdowns.

How It Works

Multi-Source Scraping

Daily automated ingestion from 8 broker networks — BizBuySell, BizQuest, BusinessBroker.net, Sunbelt, Transworld, and others. Extracts price, EBITDA, revenue, employees, industry, location, and full broker descriptions

Filtering & Dedup

Hard exclusion engine rejects 12 categories (restaurants, ecommerce, cannabis, franchises, hotels, etc.). URL-based deduplication across all sources ensures no listing is scored twice. EBITDA proxy logic falls back to cash flow or SDE when EBITDA is missing

Dual Scoring Algorithm

Each deal gets two independent 0–100 scores: Operator Score (operational simplicity, owner independence, revenue stability, customer diversification, lifestyle load) and PE Score (platform scalability, revenue quality, management depth, value creation potential, exit optionality). Each score = 50 pts qualitative text analysis + 50 pts quantitative public data

Public Data Enrichment

Integrates Census Bureau data (County Business Patterns, Business Dynamics Statistics) for industry survival rates, competition density, and market fragmentation. Pulls BLS QCEW data for 3-year employment growth trends and wage pressure analysis — all resolved to county-level FIPS codes

Geographic Intelligence

ZIP-to-county FIPS resolution with city/state fallback. Maps deals to 40+ defined markets across 5 regions with tier classifications (Tier 1/2/3). Enables market-by-market pipeline comparison and regional concentration analysis

Dashboard & Analytics

Full listing detail pages with score breakdowns for both buyer types. Badge system ranks each deal (e.g., "Platform Candidate", "Operator Ideal"). Filterable by region, market, score, EBITDA band, and industry. Tracks recently sold listings and lifecycle changes. Auto-deploys daily via Vercel

Key Highlights

  • 5,000+ active listings scored across 8 marketplace sources
  • Dual algorithmic scoring — independent frameworks for solo buyers vs. PE sponsors
  • Public data integration: Census Bureau (CBP, BDS) + Bureau of Labor Statistics (QCEW)
  • 300+ keyword text analysis engine extracts qualitative signals from broker descriptions
  • Zero-touch daily pipeline — scrape, score, deploy with no manual intervention

Pharmacy M&A Intelligence Platform

A full-stack acquisition targeting platform that ingests 112,000+ U.S. pharmacy records from the CMS NPI registry, enriches with Medicare Part D claims and Census demographics, classifies chain vs. independent, scores acquisition readiness using a 6-factor weighted model, and tracks deals through an 8-stage pipeline.

Problem

Pharmacy acquisition teams manually sift through fragmented public databases to find independent targets. There's no centralized tool that combines provider registry data, prescription volume, Medicare/340B designation, demographics, and competitive density into a single scoring system — and no way to probabilistically identify which stores are most likely to be available at a discount.

Solution

An automated pipeline that downloads the full NPPES registry (~700MB, 10GB+ expanded), filters across 12 pharmacy taxonomy codes, classifies 112,000+ pharmacies as chain vs. independent, enriches with Medicare Part D claims and ZIP-level Census demographics, applies a weighted acquisition score, surfaces closing signals and retirement risk probabilities, and delivers everything in a filterable dashboard with a built-in deal tracker.

How It Works

NPI Registry Ingestion

Downloads the full CMS NPPES data dissemination file (~700MB compressed, 10GB+ expanded). Parses in 100K-row chunks via Pandas, filters on 12 pharmacy-specific taxonomy codes, and batch-inserts 5,000 records at a time. Covers 112,000+ pharmacies across all 50 states and territories

Chain Classification Engine

Regex matching against 35+ named chains (CVS, Walgreens, Walmart, Rite Aid, Kroger, Costco, Genoa, etc.). Flags institutional pharmacies (hospitals, nursing, LTC, correctional). Multi-location clustering automatically identifies operators with 10+ locations as de facto chains. Parses ownership signals (LLC, Inc, Partnership, PC) from organization names

Medicare & 340B Enrichment

Enriches pharmacies with Medicare Part D claims data — total claims filled, unique beneficiaries served, and total drug cost. Identifies 340B-eligible pharmacies (federally qualified health centers, critical access hospitals, contract pharmacies) which fill prescriptions at deeply discounted acquisition costs — a key signal for file value and competitive positioning

Census Demographic Scoring

Pulls ZIP-level U.S. Census data for every pharmacy: population, % age 65+ (seniors fill 2–3x more scripts), median household income (payer mix quality), and population growth rate. Calculates local competition density — pharmacies per 10,000 residents — to identify underserved markets where acquisition targets hold more value

6-Factor Acquisition Score

Composite 0–100 score with weighted factors: Rx Volume (30%) — estimated scripts/year scaled to 80K baseline; Competition (20%) — pharmacies per 10K population; Aging Population (20%) — % 65+ scaled to 30% threshold; Retirement Risk (15%) — years in operation from NPI enumeration date; Income (8%) — median ZIP income; Pop Growth (7%) — market expansion signal

Closing Signals & Probability

Identifies pharmacies with a high likelihood of being available or discounted using three signals: Long-tenured owners (20+ years operating — likely near retirement); Stale NPI records (3+ years without update — possible inactivity); Nearby deactivations (pharmacies in ZIPs where others recently closed — displaced patients need a new provider). Change detection tracks deactivations, new registrations, and field-level updates across pipeline runs

Key Highlights

  • 112,000+ pharmacies ingested, classified, and scored across all 50 states
  • Multi-source enrichment: CMS NPI registry, Medicare Part D claims, 340B designation, U.S. Census demographics
  • 6-factor weighted acquisition score with retirement risk probability and closing signal detection
  • Change detection system snapshots state before each run and tracks field-level deltas
  • 8-stage deal pipeline tracker (Prospect to Closed) with contact management and CSV export
  • Dockerized full-stack deployment — FastAPI, React, PostgreSQL in one command

AI-Powered Travel Planner

A personalized travel recommendation engine with a curated database of 326 cities across 84 countries — each with 28 data fields. A 9-step preference wizard captures 40+ user inputs, feeds them into a 30-factor scoring algorithm, then auto-generates multi-city itineraries with day-by-day activities, animated route maps, and full budget breakdowns.

Problem

Planning multi-city international trips is overwhelming. Travelers spend hours researching destinations, comparing costs, checking seasons, and building itineraries — often ending up with generic recommendations that don't match their actual budget, comfort level, interests, or dietary needs.

Solution

A guided 9-step wizard collects detailed preferences (budget, pace, interests, comfort, safety priority, language comfort, dietary restrictions, deal-breakers), then a 30-factor scoring algorithm evaluates all 326 cities simultaneously — balancing budget fit, seasonal weather, flight duration, adventure level, crowd tolerance, and 20+ other dimensions to produce a personalized, multi-country itinerary in seconds.

How It Works

9-Step Preference Wizard

Captures 40+ inputs across 9 sections: trip basics ($500–$50K budget, 3–60 days), travel style (adventure level, pace, group type), 12 interest categories, 12 must-have experiences (scuba, safari, aurora, etc.), comfort & dietary needs, destination filters (city size, landscape, beach type), social preferences, safety/logistics, and deal-breakers

30-Factor Scoring Algorithm

Each city scored on: interest tag matching (+15 pts each), budget fit (+25/−penalty), seasonal weather alignment (+15), comfort level scaling, adventure compatibility, flight duration penalties, jet lag sensitivity (−hours × 1.5), language comfort (+8), safety priority (+8), city size preference, landscape/terrain matching, nightlife scoring, crowd tolerance, WiFi needs, dietary restrictions, cultural immersion, shopping, fitness level, and must-have experience matching (+12 exact, +4 tag)

Multi-Country Routing

Dynamically determines optimal country count based on trip length (1–4 countries). Scores countries by composite average of their top 3 cities, then applies regional proximity filtering — additional countries must be in the same region or score above 60% of the top pick. Selects 2–3 cities per country and calculates inter-region flight hours to minimize total travel time

Day Allocation & Activities

Calculates a "richness score" per city (how many user interests it matches) and distributes available days proportionally — richer cities get more time. Generates 3–5 activities per day based on chosen pace (Relaxed/Moderate/Packed) using a templating system with 121 curated activity templates across 12 interest categories. Deduplication ensures no activity repeats within a trip

Interactive Route Map

Plotly animated scatter-geo map plots the full route on a natural earth projection. Marker sizes scale with days allocated. Animated frames reveal each stop sequentially with play/pause controls and a timeline slider. Flight vs. overland connections shown with distinct line styles. All 326 cities available on a separate explore map, color-coded by region with hover details

Budget Engine & Tools

Estimates total trip cost using per-city daily rates, Airbnb averages, Uber costs, and group size multipliers — broken down by city with visual summaries. Additional tools: side-by-side city comparison (up to 3 cities), top-ranked destination lists, budget calculator, and a "Surprise Me" random generator for spontaneous planning. User accounts with SHA-256 hashed auth for saving multiple trips

Key Highlights

  • 326 cities, 84 countries — each with 28 data fields including curated neighborhoods, top activities, local cuisine, festivals, and Airbnb/Uber costs
  • 30-factor scoring algorithm balancing budget, season, safety, language, dietary needs, crowd tolerance, and 20+ other dimensions
  • Intelligent multi-country routing with proximity filtering and richness-based day allocation
  • 121 activity templates across 12 interest categories with pace-aware scheduling and deduplication
  • Animated Plotly route map with play/pause, timeline slider, and region-coded explore map
