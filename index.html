PE Deal Intelligence Dashboard

A deal intelligence platform that aggregates listings from business-for-sale marketplaces, runs a dual scoring algorithm (Operator vs. PE buyer), enriches with Census Bureau and BLS labor data, and surfaces ranked opportunities in an interactive dashboard with PDF deal reports and analytics.

Problem

PE deal sourcing is manual and slow. Analysts scan fragmented platforms, copy data into spreadsheets, and apply inconsistent criteria. Good deals get buried — and there's no way to objectively compare an owner-operator opportunity against a PE platform target using real market data.

Solution

An automated pipeline that aggregates deal flow from 8 broker networks, deduplicates across sources, runs two independent 0–100 scoring frameworks (solo buyer vs. PE sponsor), enriches each deal with public market data from the Census Bureau and Bureau of Labor Statistics, and delivers a ranked pipeline with full scoring breakdowns, an interactive deal map, and exportable PDF reports.

How It Works

Deal Flow Aggregation

Aggregates and normalizes deal flow from 8 broker marketplace sources on a daily automated schedule across 35 states

  • 8 marketplace sources monitored daily — extracts asking price, EBITDA/cash flow/SDE, gross revenue, employee count, industry classification, location (city/state/ZIP), and full broker descriptions
  • 35 states covered across all U.S. regions: Midwest, Southeast, Northeast, Southwest, West Coast
  • 2,300+ total deals reviewed through the pipeline — only the highest-quality pass through to the dashboard
  • Listings stored in SQLite with first-seen / last-seen date tracking. Deals that disappear are flagged as "likely sold" for market intelligence
  • Zero manual intervention — runs daily via automated scheduler
Filtering & Classification

Exclusion engine rejects low-quality deal types, deduplicates across sources, and applies EBITDA eligibility thresholds

  • 12 hard-exclude categories: Food/Nightlife, Ecommerce/FBA, Cannabis/Vape/Liquor, Crypto/Forex, Gas Stations, Hotels/Lodging, Healthcare Reimbursement, Environmental Risk, Commodity Labor, Low-Defensibility Retail, Real Estate Only, Franchises
  • EBITDA minimums: $400K for Operator track, $500K for PE track
  • EBITDA proxy chain: Prefers explicit EBITDA → falls back to cash flow → SDE. A −5 pt scoring penalty applied when using proxy values
  • Dedup: URL-based matching across all 8 sources — each listing tracked in a seen_urls set to ensure 1:1 uniqueness
  • Valuation multiples auto-calculated (price / EBITDA) for every listing with earnings data
Dual Scoring Algorithm

Two independent 0–100 scores per deal — one for solo/owner-operator buyers, one for PE sponsors. Each = 50 pts qualitative + 50 pts quantitative

  • Operator — Qualitative (50 pts): Operational Simplicity (15), Owner Independence (10), Revenue Stability (10), Customer Diversification (10), Lifestyle Load (5). Penalizes owner-dependent, seasonal, 24/7 businesses. Rewards recurring revenue and diversified customers
  • Operator — Quantitative (50 pts): Industry Survival Rate (15 — Census BDS firm death rate), Local Demand (10 — BLS employment growth), Labor Cost Pressure (10 — wage growth differential), Competition Density (10 — establishments per capita), Debt Safety (5 — leverage + margin)
  • PE — Qualitative (50 pts): Platform Scalability (15 — multi-location, 25+ employees, $10M+ revenue), Revenue Quality (10), Management Depth (10), Value Creation Potential (10), Exit Optionality (5 — NAICS on 54-sector PE whitelist)
  • PE — Quantitative (50 pts): Market Fragmentation (15 — % of market <20 employees), Market Growth (10 — blended 60% local + 40% national), Labor Efficiency (10), Add-on Pipeline (10 — establishment density per 100K), Financing Resilience (5)
  • Text analysis: 300+ keywords scanned across title, description, and broker notes with hard/soft penalty tiers per bucket
Public Data Enrichment

Integrates Census Bureau and BLS data to score each deal against real industry and labor market conditions at the county level

  • Census CBP: Establishment count, employment, payroll, and size distribution by NAICS + county. Fragmentation analysis (% of firms <20 employees). 4-digit NAICS with 2-digit fallback
  • Census BDS: Firm death rate by industry — thresholds: ≤8% → 15 pts, ≤10% → 12, ≤12% → 9, ≤14% → 6, ≤16% → 3
  • BLS QCEW: 3-year annualized employment and wage growth at county level. Private sector only. National data as fallback
  • NAICS inference: Automatic industry classification from listing text when not explicitly provided
  • Caching: SQLite 30-day TTL cache keyed by naics:fips:is_county. Data confidence flags: High/Medium/Low/None with scoring penalties for lower confidence
Interactive Deal Map

Leaflet-based U.S. map with market-level deal visualization, click-to-filter popups, and regional concentration analysis across 40+ markets

  • Market dots: Color-coded by average deal score, sized by deal count. Click any market to see avg score, deal count, avg EBITDA, avg multiple

  • Click-through from map popup directly to filtered listings for that market
Dashboard, Analytics & PDF Export

Full dashboard with badge rankings, analytics page, SBA financing calculator, and exportable 2-page PDF deal reports

  • Two-track system: Users select "Solo Search" or "PE Fund" at login — all stats, listings, and filters adapt to the chosen track
  • Badge system — Operator: "Operator Ideal" (90+), "Strong Owner Business" (80+), "Manageable With Risk" (70+), "High Operator Load" (60+), "Avoid" (<60)
  • Badge system — PE: "Elite Platform Target" (90+), "Platform Candidate" (80+), "Add-On Target" (70+), "Sponsor Review" (60+), "Low PE Priority" (<60)
  • Analytics page: Score distributions, deals by region, top industries, EBITDA band analysis, source breakdown, recently sold tracking (Chart.js)
  • SBA Calculator: Seller financing support, rate/term sensitivity table, affordability back-solver, extra payment prepayment savings
  • PDF export: 2-page professional report — deal summary + financial snapshot on page 1, full 10-bucket scoring breakdown on page 2. Track-specific analysis (IC memo for solo, LBO analysis for PE)
  • Lifecycle: First-seen / last-seen dates, recently sold detection, source monitoring across all networks

Pharmacy M&A Intelligence Platform

A full-stack acquisition targeting platform that ingests 112,000+ U.S. pharmacy records from the CMS NPI registry, enriches with Medicare Part D claims and Census demographics, classifies chain vs. independent, scores acquisition readiness using a 6-factor weighted model, and tracks deals through an 8-stage pipeline.

Problem

Pharmacy acquisition teams manually sift through fragmented public databases to find independent targets. There's no centralized tool that combines provider registry data, prescription volume, Medicare/340B designation, demographics, and competitive density into a single scoring system — and no way to probabilistically identify which stores are most likely to be available at a discount.

Solution

An automated pipeline that downloads the full NPPES registry (~700MB, 10GB+ expanded), filters across 12 pharmacy taxonomy codes, classifies 112,000+ pharmacies as chain vs. independent, enriches with Medicare Part D claims and ZIP-level Census demographics, applies a weighted acquisition score, surfaces closing signals and retirement risk probabilities, and delivers everything in a filterable dashboard with a built-in deal tracker.

How It Works

NPI Registry Ingestion

Downloads and processes the full CMS NPPES registry — 112,000+ pharmacies across all 50 states and territories

  • Source: CMS NPPES Data Dissemination — downloaded via httpx streaming in 1MB chunks with a 1-hour timeout
  • Parsing: Pandas read_csv with chunksize=100,000 rows and low_memory=False. Entity type filter (organizations only). Batch inserts of 5,000 records per database commit
  • 12 pharmacy taxonomy codes: Community/Retail, Compounding, Long Term Care, Home Infusion, Institutional, Mail Order, Military, Managed Care, Nuclear, Specialty, General Pharmacy, and Pharmacist
  • Normalization: Phone standardized to (XXX) XXX-XXXX, names uppercased, ZIP truncated to 5 digits, dedup key = MD5 hash of org_name|address|zip
  • NPI date extraction: Enumeration date, last update date, deactivation date/reason parsed from columns 36–39. Years in operation calculated from enumeration date
  • Coverage: 64 states/territories. Top 5 independent: NY (6,827), CA (5,844), TX (5,502), FL (5,248), MI (2,819)
Chain Classification Engine

Identifies and separates chains, independents, and institutional pharmacies using pattern matching and multi-location clustering

  • Named chains matched: CVS, Walgreens, Walmart, Rite Aid, Kroger, Costco, Sam's Club, Target, Publix, H-E-B, Albertsons, Safeway, Meijer, Winn-Dixie, Giant, ShopRite, Wegmans, Hy-Vee, Fred Meyer, Harris Teeter, Omnicare, PharMerica, Kindred, BrightSpring, Cardinal Health, McKesson, AmerisourceBergen, Express Scripts, Optum Rx, Cigna, Amazon Pharmacy, Capsule, Alto, Genoa, PharmHouse
  • Multi-location clustering: Any organization name appearing at 10+ distinct addresses is auto-flagged as a chain — catches regional operators not in the named chain list (~18,000 pharmacies)
  • Institutional filtering: Regex patterns for: Hospital, Medical Center, Nursing, Long-Term Care, LTC, Skilled Nursing, Rehab, Assisted Living, Infusion, Correctional, Prison, Veterans, VA — tagged but not deleted from database
  • Ownership signals: Corporation (41,764), LLC (30,449), Unknown (39,174), Partnership (935), Professional Corporation (215)
  • Result: 65,279 independents (58%), 47,258 chains (42%), 3,890 institutional
Medicare & 340B Enrichment

Enriches pharmacies with Medicare Part D claims data and identifies 340B-eligible pharmacies — key signals for file value and acquisition pricing

  • Medicare Part D fields: Total claims count (prescriptions filled), unique Medicare beneficiaries served, total drug cost in dollars — sourced from CMS prescriber-level utilization data
  • 340B program: Federal drug pricing program that requires drug manufacturers to sell outpatient drugs at a discount to eligible healthcare organizations. 340B pharmacies acquire drugs at 25–50% below retail — dramatically affecting per-script margin and file value calculations
  • 340B eligibility signals: Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs), critical access hospitals, disproportionate share hospitals, contract pharmacy arrangements — identified via taxonomy codes and organization name patterns
  • Why it matters for M&A: A pharmacy's 340B status signals margin compression risk for buyers — or opportunity if the acquirer can maintain the 340B contract. Medicare volume indicates patient stickiness and reimbursement predictability
Census Demographic Scoring

Enriches every pharmacy with ZIP-level Census data — aging population, income, growth, and competition density — to identify high-value markets

  • Coverage: 99.8% of pharmacies enriched with Census data (112,154 of 112,537)
  • % Age 65+: Range 0–100%, average 17.93%. Scaled to a 30% threshold (30% or higher = max score of 100). Rationale: seniors fill 2–3x more prescriptions and have higher adherence — directly correlates with script volume and file value
  • Median household income: Range $2,499–$250,001, average $81,776. Scaled to $100K threshold. Higher income = better commercial payer mix = higher reimbursement per script
  • Population growth: Weighted multiplier of 5x applied (e.g., 10% growth → 100 pts). Signals expanding patient base and market demand
  • Competition density: Pharmacies per 10,000 residents per ZIP. Range 0.17–10,000, average 6.65. Thresholds: ≤1 → 100 pts (excellent), ≤3 → 80, ≤5 → 60, ≤8 → 40, ≤12 → 20, >12 → 10 (saturated)
  • File value estimate: Rx volume × $4/script (industry standard acquisition price). Total independent pipeline value: ~$4.72 billion
6-Factor Acquisition Score

Composite 0–100 score combining Rx volume, competition, aging population, retirement risk, income, and population growth

  • Rx Volume (30%): Estimated scripts/year scaled to 80,000 baseline = 100 pts. Range across database: 1,732–123,750 scripts/year, average 18,073. Default 20 pts if volume unknown
  • Competition (20%): Based on zip_pharmacies_per_10k. ≤1 → 100, ≤3 → 80, ≤5 → 60, ≤8 → 40, ≤12 → 20, >12 → 10
  • Aging Population (20%): % residents 65+ scaled to 30% ceiling. Average score strong due to 17.93% national average
  • Retirement Risk (15%): Based on years since NPI enumeration. ≥25 yrs → 100 (owner likely 60+), ≥20 → 80, ≥15 → 50, ≥10 → 30, <10 → 10. 3,435 pharmacies currently at 20+ years
  • Income (8%): Median ZIP income / $100,000 × 100. Caps at 100
  • Pop Growth (7%): 50 + (growth_pct × 5). 0% growth = 50 pts, 10% growth = 100 pts
  • Final score distribution: Range 9.69–87.48, average 44.41 across all 112,537 pharmacies
Closing Signals & Probability

Surfaces pharmacies with a high likelihood of being available or discounted based on retirement risk, record staleness, and nearby closures

  • Long-tenured (20+ years): 3,435 pharmacies. Owner likely age 60+ and approaching retirement — highest probability of willingness to sell. NPI enumeration date used as proxy for business age
  • Stale NPI records (3+ years): 71,986 pharmacies. No update to CMS registration in 3+ years may indicate declining engagement, reduced operations, or owner disengagement — early signal of potential availability
  • Nearby deactivations: 155 pharmacies recently deactivated in the system. Platform surfaces independent pharmacies in the same ZIP code — these stores inherit displaced patient volume, making them higher-value targets (and their owners may be motivated by the same market pressures)
  • Change detection: Snapshots full database state before each pipeline run. Compares new vs. previous records to detect new registrations, field updates, and deactivations. Stores audit trail with change type, field name, old value, and new value
  • Pipeline tracking: Each run logs started_at, completed_at, status, records_processed, records_added, records_updated, and changes_detected

Key Highlights

  • 112,000+ pharmacies ingested, classified, and scored across all 50 states
  • Multi-source enrichment: CMS NPI registry, Medicare Part D claims, 340B designation, U.S. Census demographics
  • 6-factor weighted acquisition score with retirement risk probability and closing signal detection
  • Change detection system snapshots state before each run and tracks field-level deltas
  • 8-stage deal pipeline tracker (Prospect to Closed) with contact management and CSV export
  • Dockerized full-stack deployment — FastAPI, React, PostgreSQL in one command

AI-Powered Travel Planner

A full-featured travel planning platform with 326 cities across 84 countries. A 9-step preference wizard feeds into a 30-factor scoring algorithm that generates personalized multi-city itineraries with structured activities, animated route maps, visa info, booking links, smart packing lists, budget breakdowns, and exportable PDF trip reports.

Problem

Planning multi-city international trips is overwhelming. Travelers spend hours researching destinations, comparing costs, checking seasons, and building itineraries — often ending up with generic recommendations that don't match their actual budget, comfort level, interests, or dietary needs.

Solution

A guided 9-step wizard collects detailed preferences (budget, pace, interests, comfort, safety priority, language comfort, dietary restrictions, deal-breakers), then a 30-factor scoring algorithm evaluates all 326 cities simultaneously — balancing budget fit, seasonal weather, flight duration, adventure level, crowd tolerance, and 20+ other dimensions to produce a personalized, multi-country itinerary in seconds.

How It Works

9-Step Preference Wizard

Guided questionnaire captures 40+ inputs across budget, travel style, interests, must-have experiences, comfort, and deal-breakers

  • Step 1 — Trip Basics: Budget ($500–$50K), duration (3–60 days), departure city, start/end dates, travel month (auto-detected), multi-country toggle
  • Step 2 — Travel Style: Adventure level (1–100 slider), pace (Relaxed/Moderate/Packed), group type (Solo/Couple/Group/Family), trip purpose (honeymoon, bucket list, gap year, etc.), travel experience level
  • Step 3 — Interests: 12 categories: food, temples, nightlife, beaches, hiking, art, shopping, architecture, wellness, wildlife, photography, local culture
  • Step 4 — Experiences: 12 must-haves: scuba diving, volcanoes, aurora borealis, safari, wine tasting, hot air balloons, mountain trekking, cooking classes, scenic train journeys, river cruises, festivals, ancient ruins
  • Step 5 — Comfort: Comfort level (1–100), transport mode, food adventurousness, dietary restrictions, WiFi needs, fitness level
  • Steps 6–9: City size preference, landscape/beach type, language comfort, cultural immersion, nightlife importance, crowd tolerance, safety priority, jet lag tolerance (3 modes), shopping importance, weather preference, region preference, long-flight deal-breaker
30-Factor Scoring Algorithm

Every city evaluated against the user's preferences across 30 weighted dimensions — from budget and weather to dietary needs and crowd tolerance

  • Interest matching: +15 pts per tag match across 12 categories (food, temples, nightlife, beaches, hiking, art, shopping, architecture, wellness, wildlife, photography, culture)
  • Budget fit: +25 if daily cost ≤ budget per person; +10 if within 120% of budget; dynamic penalty −(overage × 0.3) if over budget
  • Season/weather: +15 if travel month is in city's "good months"; −5 if outside. Warm-only preference: +10 match / −15 mismatch. No-rain preference: −10 for tropical climates
  • Comfort & adventure: +8 if city comfort ≥ user level; dynamic penalty −(gap × 5) for mismatch. Adventure ≥70 with hiking/wildlife tags: +5 bonus. Adventure ≤30 with high comfort: +5 bonus
  • Flight & jet lag: Long-haul penalty: −20 for >10 hrs, −10 for >8 hrs (if deal-breaker enabled). Jet lag: −(hours × 1.5) in minimize mode; −8 if prefer_few and >10 hrs
  • Location preferences: Language comfort (+8/+3), safety priority (+8/+2), city size match (+8/−8), landscape/terrain (+10), beach type (+6/−3), nightlife (+6/−6), crowd tolerance (+3/−8)
  • Lifestyle factors: WiFi needs (+5), dietary/vegetarian (+5/−5), cultural immersion (+5), shopping (+5), fitness/hiking (+5/−5), photography (+5)
  • Must-have experiences: +12 pts for exact city match (e.g., Tromso for aurora), +4 pts for tag match. 12 experience types with 8–12 curated cities each
  • Random noise: ±5 pts added for natural variance — ensures different recommendations across runs
Multi-Country Routing

Determines optimal country and city count based on trip length, then applies proximity filtering to minimize travel time

  • Country count logic: Multi-country mode: max(2, min(4, trip_days ÷ 5)). Single mode: 1–7 days → 1 country, 8–14 → 2, 15+ → 3
  • Country scoring: Each country scored by the average of its top 3 cities' individual scores
  • Proximity filtering: Additional countries must be in the same region as the top pick, or score above 60% of the top country's score to qualify cross-region
  • Cities per country: min(3, max(2, trip_days ÷ (num_countries × 3))). Example: 10-day, 2-country trip → 2 cities per country
  • Inter-region flight hours: NA↔Asia: 14 hrs, NA↔Europe: 8 hrs, within Europe: 3 hrs, within Asia: 4 hrs — used to penalize impractical multi-region trips
  • 6 regions: Asia, Europe, South America, Africa, Oceania, North America — each color-coded on the explore map
Day Allocation & Activities

Distributes days proportionally by interest richness and generates structured activities with Google Maps links, hours, and costs

  • Richness formula: richness = count of matching interest tags between user preferences and city data. Minimum richness = 1 to guarantee every city gets at least 1 day
  • Day distribution: available_days = trip_days − travel_days (1 travel day per inter-city move). Each city gets: (city_richness ÷ total_richness) × available_days, rounded. Remainder days added to first city; excess trimmed from last cities (min 1 day each)
  • Activity pool per city: 1) All pre-curated city highlights, 2) templated activities for each matching interest tag, 3) generic fallback activities. Drawn from without replacement to prevent repeats
  • 121 activity templates across 12 categories. Examples: "Street food walking tour in {city}", "Hike to the best scenic viewpoint in {city}", "Book a traditional spa treatment in {city}"
  • Pacing: Relaxed = 3 activities/day, Moderate = 4, Packed = 5. Time slots: Morning → Midday → Afternoon → Evening → Night
  • If activity pool is exhausted: fallback to "Free time to explore {city}"
Interactive Route Map

Animated Plotly map reveals the route stop-by-stop with play/pause controls, plus a global explore map of all 326 cities

  • Projection: Natural earth with light gray land, light blue ocean, and visible coastlines
  • Markers: Departure city = gold star (size 16). Route cities = blue circles, size = max(10, days_allocated × 5). White outline, 0.9 opacity
  • Route lines: Cross-country flights = blue dashed (#3498db, width 2.5). Same-country overland = teal solid (#2ec4b6, width 2)
  • Animation: One frame per city in route sequence. Frame duration: 1000ms, transition: 500ms. Play/pause buttons + scrubbing slider (300ms step duration)
  • Explore map: All 326 cities plotted. Color-coded by region (red=Asia, blue=Europe, green=S. America, orange=Africa, purple=Oceania). Hover shows: cost/day, best months, interest tags, highlights, and description
Budget, Booking & Export

Full budget breakdowns, direct booking links, visa info, smart packing lists, and exportable PDF trip reports

  • Budget engine: Per city: daily_rate × days_allocated × group_size. Includes Airbnb averages ($25–$220+/night), Uber costs ($2–$30), and per-person daily rates — all curated for 326 cities
  • Booking links: Direct integration with Kiwi.com (flights), Booking.com (hotels), and Airbnb — auto-fills dates and destination
  • Visa info: Color-coded visa badges per country — visa-free, visa-on-arrival, e-visa, or embassy required — with max stay duration and notes
  • Practical info panel: Currency, power plugs, tipping customs, emergency numbers, SIM card advice, tap water safety, and dress codes for every destination
  • Smart packing list: Auto-generated based on destinations, climate, terrain, and activities — with checkboxes for tracking
  • PDF export: Polished multi-page trip report with title page, daily itinerary, city info cards, budget summary, and practical info — styled with colored headers and visual hierarchy
  • Tools: Side-by-side city comparison (up to 3), top-ranked lists, budget calculator, and "Surprise Me" random generator
  • User accounts: SHA-256 hashed auth with salt. Save multiple trip plans per account
